# CEDA Strategic Framework: The 3P Approach

**People · Policy · Process**

---

## Overview

CEDA's strategy is organised around three interconnected pillars — **People, Policy, and Platforms** — that together drive the organisation's mission of advancing extractives governance, energy transition, and accountability in Ghana. Each pillar operates across national and community levels and is reinforced by strategic partnerships with government, civil society, and development partners.

---

## The Three Pillars

### 1. People

*Building the human capital and public awareness needed for accountable governance.*

- Capacity building for citizens, communities, and institutional stakeholders
- Awareness creation on extractives governance, energy transition, and anti-corruption
- Civic engagement at the community level to strengthen local voice and participation

### 2. Policy

*Shaping the rules and institutions that govern Ghana's extractives and energy sectors.*

- Policy analysis, legal review, and reform advocacy
- National-level engagement with government bodies and regulatory institutions
- Strategic partnerships with CSOs, development partners, and accountability mechanisms (EITI/GHEITI, PIAC, etc.)

### 3. Process

*Creating the data, tools, and systems that power evidence-based advocacy.*

- Data generation and research to inform policy positions and public discourse
- Technology partnerships (including collaboration with SYND) for data infrastructure
- Knowledge products and digital channels that amplify CEDA's reach and impact

---

## Strategic Spotlight: Energy Transition & Critical Minerals

The energy transition represents a cross-cutting priority across all three pillars — building informed communities (**People**), shaping national frameworks for critical minerals governance (**Policy**), and generating the evidence base to guide Ghana's transition pathway (**Process**).
