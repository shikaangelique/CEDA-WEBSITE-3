# CEDA Governing Board

The Governing Board provides strategic direction, sets organisational policy, monitors compliance, approves annual plans and budgets, and reviews the quality of CEDA's work. The board is composed of leaders drawn from law, energy and extractives policy, governance reform, and international development, with the Executive Director sitting as a permanent member and Board Secretary.

---

## Juliette Twumasi-Anokye
*Board Chair*

Juliette is a dual-qualified lawyer in England, Wales, and Ghana, bringing over 20 years of experience in competition law, energy policy, and regulatory governance. She currently serves as the Chairperson of the ECOWAS Regional Competition Authority, Managing Partner of Anojul & Associates, and Co-Founder and Principal Consultant of Anojul Afriyie & Co. Ltd.

Her distinguished career spans both regional and international roles, including serving as a Consultant for Local Content at the Petroleum Commission of Ghana (2012–2017) and as a Technical Consultant at the ECOWAS Regional Competition Authority (2022–2023). She has also held key leadership and advisory positions such as Senior Legal Counsel at the Ministry of Energy, Deputy Director and subsequently Principal at the Postal Services Commission in the United Kingdom (2005–2011), and Principal Case Officer at the UK Office of Fair Trading (2000–2005).

We are excited to benefit from her extensive expertise and strategic insight as we continue to advance our mission.

---

## Benjamin Aryee
*Board Member*

Ben is one of Ghana's most respected voices in mining policy and resource governance, with several decades of experience shaping the country's extractive sector.

He currently chairs the Policy Review Committee leading the comprehensive overhaul of Ghana's 2014 Mining Policy and the Minerals and Mining Act, 2006 (Act 703), driving the integration of critical minerals, climate, and ESG considerations into the new regulatory framework.

He previously served as Chief Executive Officer of the Minerals Commission of Ghana, and subsequently as Special Advisor to the Minister for Lands and Natural Resources. His career has consistently centred on local content, value addition, and ensuring that Ghana's mineral wealth circulates within the domestic economy. He is also the author of *Historical Overview of Ghana's Mining Sector Fiscal Regimes*.

We are honoured to draw on his deep institutional memory and continued leadership in mining sector reform as we advance our mission.

---

## Pauline Anaman
*Board Member*

Pauline is a transactions lawyer and green transition strategist with over 13 years of experience, whose work supports governments, businesses, and investors in navigating risks and unlocking long-term value within Africa's emerging green economy.

With over a decade of experience across Africa's energy and extractives sectors, she brings deep expertise in analysing how the global shift to a low-carbon economy is reshaping regulatory frameworks, financing models, and commercial opportunities across the continent.

Pauline currently coordinates the Green Transition Practice at AB & David Africa, a leading global business law firm with a pan-African focus. From 2020 to 2024, she served as an External Advisor on Africa to the European Union's €5.8 million Horizon 2020 Negative Emissions Project (NEGEM), where she was the sole African representative.

Prior to this, Pauline was Head of Policy at the Africa Centre for Energy Policy (ACEP), where she established and led the Climate Change and Energy Transition Unit.

We look forward to the strategic insight and leadership she brings as we continue to advance our mission.

## Chris Aston
*Board Member*

Chris is an international development advisor with over fourteen years of experience in donor change programmes.

He currently serves as the team leader of an artisanal and small-scale gold mining-focused UK Integrated Security Fund (ISF) Serious and Organised Crime (SOC) programme in Ghana, and Reporting and Evaluation Lead for a UK Conflict, Stability and Security Fund (CSSF) SOC programme in Nigeria, under TAG International. He previously served as the MREL Lead Advisor on a multi-year UK government funded project in Georgia, and on a nine-month FCDO maritime security research and analysis project covering Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and the Maldives.

Between 2019 and 2025, Chris worked with Adam Smith International, where he held various roles including Monitoring & Evaluation Service Manager, Transparency & Accountability Lead, and Monitoring & Evaluation Advisor.

Prior to this, Chris served as an ISF Cyber Theory of Change Advisor, and as a Monitoring and Evaluation Advisor for Integrity and Tetra Tech Coffey respectively.

We look forward to the depth and rigour his evaluation expertise brings as we continue to advance our mission.

## Rushaiya Ibrahim-Tanko
*Board Member*

Rushaiya is a lawyer and policy analyst with over 14 years of experience working in energy, governance, and economic growth. She currently serves as the Global Policy Director at the Energy for Growth Hub, where she previously led the PPA Transparency Project from 2021 to 2025.

Her professional experience spans legal practice, policy advisory, and development programming. At Kasser Law Firm, she worked on civil and criminal litigation while also providing consultancy services to local and international organisations on social protection policy, legal analysis, capacity building for public sector workers, and issues such as child trafficking and child labour.

From 2017 to 2020, she served as the Accountability and Beneficial Ownership Transparency Advisor on the DFID-funded Strengthening Action Against Corruption (STAAC-Ghana) Project at Adam Smith International. She has also held multiple roles at the Natural Resource Governance Institute, including Ghana Office Manager, African Program Assistant, and Africa Associate.

We look forward to the impact of her expertise and leadership as we continue to strengthen our work.

## Samuel Bekoe
*Executive Director and Board Secretary*

Samuel is the Executive Director of CEDA and serves as Board Secretary. He leads the organisation's strategic direction, governance, and external partnerships, with particular focus on extractives revenue governance, anti-corruption, and illicit financial flows.
